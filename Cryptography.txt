1::What is cryptography?::Cryptography is the science of securing information.::It ensures confidentiality, integrity, authentication, and non-repudiation.
2::Why cryptography is needed?::To protect data from unauthorized access.::Used in networks, storage, and communications.
3::Main goals of cryptography?::Confidentiality, Integrity, Authentication, Non-repudiation.::These form the security foundation.
4::What is plaintext?::Original readable data.::Input to encryption.
5::What is ciphertext?::Encrypted unreadable data.::Output of encryption.
6::What is encryption?::Converting plaintext to ciphertext.::Prevents unauthorized reading.
7::What is decryption?::Converting ciphertext back to plaintext.::Requires correct key.
8::What is a cryptographic key?::Secret value used in encryption/decryption.::Security depends on key secrecy.
9::Types of cryptography?::Symmetric, Asymmetric, Hashing.::Used for different purposes.
10::What is symmetric encryption?::Same key for encrypt and decrypt.::Fast and efficient.
11::Examples of symmetric algorithms?::AES, DES, 3DES.::Used in bulk encryption.
12::What is asymmetric encryption?::Uses public and private keys.::Slower but secure key exchange.
13::Examples of asymmetric algorithms?::RSA, ECC, ElGamal.::Used in secure communications.
14::What is hashing?::One-way transformation.::Used for integrity and passwords.
15::Examples of hash functions?::SHA-256, SHA-3, MD5.::MD5 is insecure.
16::What is key length?::Size of cryptographic key.::Longer keys provide more security.
17::What is brute-force attack?::Trying all possible keys.::Longer keys resist this.
18::What is cryptanalysis?::Breaking cryptographic systems.::Used to test strength.
19::What is Caesar cipher?::Shift-based substitution cipher.::Historically important but insecure.
20::What is substitution cipher?::Replaces characters systematically.::Vulnerable to frequency analysis.
21::What is transposition cipher?::Rearranges characters.::Does not change symbols.
22::What is frequency analysis?::Studying letter frequency.::Breaks simple ciphers.
23::What is modern cryptography?::Math-based secure algorithms.::Resistant to classical attacks.
24::What is AES?::Advanced Encryption Standard.::Current symmetric standard.
25::AES key sizes?::128, 192, 256 bits.::Larger means stronger.
26::What is DES?::Data Encryption Standard.::Obsolete due to small key.
27::Why DES is insecure?::56-bit key too small.::Easily brute-forced.
28::What is 3DES?::Triple DES encryption.::More secure than DES but slow.
29::What is block cipher?::Encrypts fixed-size blocks.::AES uses 128-bit blocks.
30::What is stream cipher?::Encrypts data stream.::RC4 is example.
31::What is RC4?::Stream cipher.::Deprecated due to vulnerabilities.
32::What is block cipher mode?::Defines block operation method.::Improves security.
33::Examples of block modes?::ECB, CBC, CFB, OFB, GCM.::Some are insecure.
34::Why ECB is insecure?::Patterns remain visible.::Leaks information.
35::What is CBC mode?::Uses chaining with IV.::Improves security over ECB.
36::What is IV?::Initialization Vector.::Prevents pattern repetition.
37::What is padding?::Filling last block.::Ensures block size match.
38::What is PKCS#7?::Padding standard.::Used in block ciphers.
39::What is authenticated encryption?::Encryption + integrity.::Prevents tampering.
40::What is GCM mode?::Galois Counter Mode.::Fast and secure.
41::What is MAC?::Message Authentication Code.::Ensures integrity and authenticity.
42::What is HMAC?::Hash-based MAC.::Uses secret key.
43::What is digital signature?::Cryptographic proof of authenticity.::Ensures integrity and non-repudiation.
44::How digital signature works?::Hash then encrypt with private key.::Verified using public key.
45::What is RSA?::Public-key algorithm based on factoring.::Used for encryption and signatures.
46::RSA key components?::Public key, Private key, Modulus.::Based on prime numbers.
47::What is ECC?::Elliptic Curve Cryptography.::Provides strong security with small keys.
48::Why ECC preferred?::Smaller keys, faster.::Efficient for mobile devices.
