1::What is Software Engineering?::Systematic approach to software development::Applies engineering principles to produce reliable, maintainable, and efficient software
2::Difference between software and software engineering?::Software is product, engineering is process::Engineering ensures quality, scalability, and cost control
3::What is SDLC?::Software Development Life Cycle::Defines phases for structured software development
4::Main phases of SDLC?::Requirement, Design, Implementation, Testing, Deployment, Maintenance::Each phase ensures controlled progress
5::Why SDLC is important?::Provides structure and predictability::Reduces risk and development cost
6::What is requirement engineering?::Process of gathering and analyzing requirements::Ensures correct problem understanding
7::Functional requirement?::Defines system functionality::Describes what system should do
8::Non-functional requirement?::Defines quality attributes::Includes performance, security, usability
9::What is SRS?::Software Requirements Specification::Formal document describing system requirements
10::Characteristics of good SRS?::Correct, complete, unambiguous::Prevents misunderstanding
11::What is feasibility study?::Analyzing project viability::Covers technical, economic, and operational feasibility
12::What is waterfall model?::Sequential SDLC model::Each phase completed before next
13::Waterfall model disadvantage?::Rigid and inflexible::Changes are costly
14::What is iterative model?::Repeated development cycles::Allows gradual improvement
15::What is incremental model?::System developed in parts::Each increment adds functionality
16::What is spiral model?::Risk-driven iterative model::Combines prototyping and waterfall
17::Advantage of spiral model?::Strong risk management::Suitable for large projects
18::What is Agile methodology?::Flexible and iterative development approach::Responds to change quickly
19::Agile manifesto focus?::Individuals, working software, collaboration, change::Customer satisfaction prioritized

22::Roles in Scrum?::Product Owner, Scrum Master, Development Team::Each has defined responsibilities

27::Agile vs Waterfall?::Flexible vs rigid::Agile supports change, Waterfall does not
